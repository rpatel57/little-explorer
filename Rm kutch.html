Dholavira

Dholavira is an archaeological site at Khadirbet in Bhachau Taluka of Kutch District, in the state of Gujarat in western India, which has taken its name from a modern-day village 1 kilometre south of it. This village is 165 km from Radhanpur. Dholavira site is India's most prominent archaeological site associated with the Indus Valley Civilization. The site is the larger of the two most remarkable excavations of the Harappan Civilisation dating back to about 4,500 years ago.

Kutch Museum

Kutch Museum is located in Bhuj, Kutch district of Gujarat, India. It is the oldest museum of Gujarat which was founded in 1877. It is located opposite Hamirsar Lake. The museum was earlier known as Fergusson Museum.Smritivan (can be translated as Forest of Memories), adorned as the largest memorial and museum to ever be built in modern India, covers a vast area over 470 acres. It is situated on the Bhujiyo Dungar (a small hill) in the outskirts of Bhuj.

Prag Mahal

The Prag Mahal is a 19th-century palace located next to the Aina Mahal in Bhuj, Kutch, Gujarat, India. The largest of the three palaces within the Darbargadh walled complex, 19th-century Prag Mahal is in a forlorn state, damaged by an earthquake, but is worth visiting for its grand Durbar Hall, with its glittering chandeliers, the Maharajah's taxidermied collection and gold-skirted classical statues.

Handicraft

Kachchh is world renowned for its mirrored embroideries. Most of these were traditionally stitched by village women, for themselves and their families, to create festivity, honor deities, or generate wealth.Best described as a 'cradle of craftsmanship', kutch is the land of weaving, block-printing, bandhini tie- and dye, Rogan-painting and various styles of embroidery, pottery, wood –carving, metal-crafts, shell-work and other handicrafts.Kutch is known for the colorful silken threads that decorate traditional Kanchlies and Kanjaries worn by Kutchie women. Embroidery is the most visible and recognized craft in Kutch. More than 40 styles of embroideries color the region, representing Kutch's diverse cultures, communities, and landscapes.

Kutch Folk Dances

The kutch district in Gujarat is well known for its traditional and cultural values. Kachchhi culture is famous for its creativities in arts and crafts. Different communities like nomadic and semi nomadic have kept the culture of Kachchh alive today’s modern time also.

Kutchhi dance is one of the many art forms of cultural activities. Folk dance of Kachchh exist since many years. The most famous dance forms are Gajiyo and Dandiya raas.A number of festivals are celebrated in Kachchh and these celebrations are accompanied with folk dance forms.

Gajiyo Raas

Gajiyo is Kachchhi folk song which is very famous in the Region. Women of Kachchh wear traditional cloths and perform traditional dance on this song. Gajiyo Raas is performed in a circle where rings of Kachchhi women revolve in cycles.

Sword Raas of Gadhvis

Gadhvi community of Kachchh represent the real bravery of Kachchhi people. They perform sword dance in which people from Gadhvi Community perform dance steps while sword in their both hands. They perform sword acts while dancing.

Kutchi food

Kutchi Dabeli

Dabeli literally means “pressed” in Gujarati language. It is said to be invented by one Keshavji Gabha Chudasama alias Kesha Malam, resident of Mandvi, Kachchh in the 1960s. When he started business he sold a dabeli at one anna or six paisa.

Mesukh

This is one of the India’s most famous sweets, People in North India calls it Mesuk-Paak, in South India they calls it Mesur-paak, we call it Mesukh. You just put it in your mouth & you don’t even have to chew it, it will melt itself in seconds. It is Made from Beshan, loads of Ghee, Pistachio, and Sugar.

Adadiya

Adadiya is famous Gujarati Winter special sweet. Adadiya is traditional Katchi and Kathiyawadi sweet. There are many ingredients in Adadiya which helps to keep body warm during winter.
